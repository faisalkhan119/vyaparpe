OVERVIEW

The 17 Global Goals at a Glance

1–5
PEOPLE

Goals for Human Dignity

Ending poverty & hunger, ensuring healthy lives, quality education, and gender equality for all.

1
No Poverty

End poverty in all forms — targeting 700M+ living on less than $2.15/day.

2
Zero Hunger

Achieve food security, improve nutrition, promote sustainable agriculture.

3
Good Health

Ensure healthy lives targeting maternal health, diseases & universal healthcare.

4
Quality Education

Ensure inclusive, equitable education & lifelong learning for all.

5
Gender Equality

Achieve gender equality & empower all women and girls.

6–10
PROSPERITY

Goals for Shared Growth

Clean water, affordable energy, decent work, innovation, and reducing global inequalities.

6
Clean Water

Ensure sustainable water management & sanitation for all communities.

7
Clean Energy

Universal access to affordable, reliable energy with solar & wind at center.

8
Decent Work

Promote sustained, inclusive economic growth & decent work for all.

9
Industry & Innovation

Build resilient infrastructure & foster inclusive industrialization.

10
Reduced Inequalities

Reduce inequality within & among countries through inclusive policies.

11–15
PLANET

Goals for Our Earth

Sustainable cities, responsible consumption, climate action & protecting life on land and water.

11
Sustainable Cities

Make cities inclusive, safe, resilient & sustainable for all residents.

12
Responsible Consumption

Ensure sustainable consumption & production patterns across industries.

13
Climate Action

Immediate action to combat climate change — limiting warming to 1.5°C per Paris Agreement.

14
Life Below Water

Conserve & sustainably use oceans, seas & marine resources.

15
Life On Land

Protect terrestrial ecosystems & halt biodiversity loss permanently.

16

Peace, Justice &
Strong Institutions

Peaceful and inclusive societies with transparent, accountable institutions are the backbone of sustainable progress.

WHY IT MATTERS

The Case for Sustainable Development

🌿

Environmental Protection

Protecting biodiversity, clean air, and water systems that sustain all life — for today and every generation that follows.

📈

Economic Stability

Sustainable economies grow without destroying those resources they depend on, creating planet-positive jobs.

🤝

Social Equity

Ensuring access to education, healthcare & opportunity regardless of gender, income, or geography.

🌐

Long-Term Resilience

Building systems robust enough to absorb future shocks — climate crises, pandemics & geopolitical disruptions.

Individuals

PERSONAL RESPONSIBILITY
♻️ Reduce & Recycle

Minimize single-use plastics, compost organics, opt for circular products.

💡 Cut Energy Use

Switch to LEDs, reduce standby power, prefer public transit.

🛒 Ethical Consumption

Support local businesses, sustainable brands. Avoid fast fashion.

📢 Advocate & Educate

Raise awareness, vote for green policies, engage your community.

Governments

INSTITUTIONAL RESPONSIBILITY
📜 Policy & Legislation

Binding laws on emissions caps, waste management & labor rights.

🌱 Climate Investment

Fund renewable infrastructure, set net-zero timelines, protect green corridors.

🎓 Education Systems

Integrate sustainability into national curricula at every level.

🌍 Global Commitments

Honor Paris Agreement pledges, scale ODA to developing nations.

THE ROAD AHEAD

Barriers to Achieving the SDGs

🌡️

Climate Change

Accelerating temperatures, intensifying disasters & rising seas hit the most vulnerable hardest.

📉

Poverty & Inequality

The wealth gap is widening within and between nations — undermining progress on all 17 goals.

💸

Financing Gap

Developing nations face a $4.2 trillion annual SDG funding gap with aid falling far short.

🌐

Geopolitical Fragmentation

Rising nationalism & fractured multilateralism erode the coordinated global action SDGs demand.
